The Founding Fathers

DJ Kool Herc

Born in Jamaica, Herc was exposed to genres of music and when he and his family moved to the Bronx. His passion for music grew and he began to DJ and started to throw parties and people all over the borough started attending the parties. His techniques were way ahead of his time and others were inspired by his work.

Grandmaster Flash

Flash was born in Barbados and moved to the Bronx at a young age. When he first heard of Herc he was curious to know more about the DJ techniques and he started to dig deep and he was able to perfect some DJ techniques such as cutting, scratching and mixing.

Afrika Bambaataa

Bambaataa grew up in the Bronx and as a teenager he saw a lot of gangs and how much violence the Bronx was facing. He created The Universal Zulu Nation where young kids and teenagers were able to express themselves in an artistic way. DJing, breakdancing, rapping and graffiti were part of the Zulu Nation and their goal was to bring peace and harmony. Bambaataa began to DJ and made futuristic sounds that paved the way.
